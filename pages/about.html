Our Founder's Story

The Albinism Foundation of Zambia (AFZ) was founded in 2008 by John Chiti, a pioneering advocate and musician who became the first person with albinism to gain prominence in Zambia. Rejected by his biological father at birth due to his albinism, John faced significant discrimination and challenges from an early age. Despite these obstacles, he pursued his passion for music and became an established artist.

John's personal journey with albinism, including facing discrimination and understanding the unique challenges faced by people with albinism in Zambia, led him to establish AFZ. His vision was to create a society where persons with albinism are fully integrated, protected, and empowered. As the first albinism foundation in Zambia, AFZ has been instrumental in advocating for the rights and welfare of people with albinism, raising awareness, and fighting against the discrimination and violence they often face.

Under John's leadership, AFZ has grown to become a significant voice for people with albinism in Zambia and internationally. The organization has worked tirelessly to break down barriers, provide support services, and build bridges of understanding and inclusion. John's work has been recognized with several awards, including the Commonwealth Points of Light award signed by Queen Elizabeth II, and he is a 2018 Mandela Washington Fellow (YALI USA).

Our History

2008 - Foundation

AFZ was founded by John Chiti with a mission to advocate for the rights of persons with albinism in Zambia.

2010 - First Programs

Launched our first comprehensive programs including sunscreen distribution and educational support.

2015 - National Recognition

Recognized by the Zambian government for our contributions to disability rights and community advocacy.

2020 - Expanded Reach

Extended our programs to all provinces of Zambia, serving over 500 beneficiaries annually.

Our Mission & Vision

Mission

To advocate for the rights and inclusion of persons with albinism in Zambia through education, support, and policy reform.

Vision

Core Values

Advocacy, Inclusion, Empowerment, Integrity, and Community Partnership guide everything we do.
